About this portal

MycoTrace is being shaped as a portfolio-grade, review-facing bioinformatics tool: official public data, reproducible processing, a heme-focused interpretation layer, and a usable portal for discussion.

Data sources and scope

  • Panel file: C:\Users\Usuario\Desktop\MycoTrace\data\reference\heme_gene_panel.csv
  • Panel genes: 31 | tier 1: 18, tier 2: 13

What this portal proves

  • Bioinformatics ETL: raw ClinVar to structured triage outputs.
  • Clinical awareness: VUS, conflict load, and review confidence are explicit signals.
  • Product thinking: cases, genes, and evidence are arranged for human review, not just for code completeness.
  • Safe boundaries: local, synthetic, read-only demo before any serious production ambitions.

How to publish it well

  • GitHub as the public technical home for code, README, figures, and reproducibility notes.
  • LinkedIn for the story: the bottleneck, the build, one screenshot, and the next question.
  • Warm hematology or molecular contacts for pilot-style feedback on output shape and utility.
  • Hiring managers or recruiters in bioinformatics, diagnostics, and translational genomics once the demo is stable.

How to extend it

  • Add a new case CSV in data/input/cases and rebuild the portal against it.
  • Expand the panel CSV in data/reference when a new disease area or gene set matters.
  • Add new PostgreSQL tables and expose them through the analytics bundle before showing them in HTML.
  • Keep public demos synthetic and keep any real or sensitive data in a private environment.
