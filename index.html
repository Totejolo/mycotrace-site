The problem

Public clinical variant data is valuable, but the path from raw release files to a queue a human can actually review is still messy, uneven, and slow.

Too much raw data

ClinVar is useful but large, inconsistent, and not immediately review-ready.

Review confidence varies

VUS, conflicts, stale evaluations, and low-confidence records still consume real human time.

Teams need structure

Review workflows need prioritization, filtering, context, and explainable output.

What is inside the demo

The demo is synthetic at the case level, but grounded in a real processing pipeline and a real heme-focused queue built from official ClinVar releases.
  • Download official ClinVar tab-delimited releases.
  • Prioritize records with review friction signals such as VUS, conflicts, and stale evaluations.
  • Filter the signal through a heme-focused panel with disease-area notes and tiers.
  • Match a case CSV against the curated review queue and render a review-ready portal.

How to talk about it

The safest and strongest framing is workflow support, not automated diagnosis.
"I built a bioinformatics pipeline and synthetic web demo that turn official ClinVar data into a hematology-focused variant triage workflow, with case and gene exploration designed to reduce review friction."

What to avoid saying

This protects both your credibility and the project.
  • Do not sell it as an AI doctor.
  • Do not imply clinical validation that does not exist.
  • Do not publish real patient data.
  • Do not claim it replaces a molecular board.
